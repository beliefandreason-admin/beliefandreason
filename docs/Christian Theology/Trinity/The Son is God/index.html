The Son (Jesus Christ) is God

Published on: February 2, 2025

The Bible provides overwhelming evidence that Jesus Christ is fully God sharing the same divine essence as the Father. Below are key categories that demonstrate His deity with extensive supporting verses.

📜 Key Scriptures​

Jesus is Directly Called God​

The Bible explicitly refers to Jesus as God in multiple places.

ReferenceVerse
John 1:1"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."
John 1:14"And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth."
John 20:28"Thomas answered him, ‘My Lord and my God!’"
Titus 2:13"Waiting for our blessed hope, the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ."
Hebrews 1:8"But of the Son he says, ‘Your throne, O God, is forever and ever...’"
Romans 9:5"To them belong the patriarchs, and from their race, according to the flesh, is the Christ, who is God over all, blessed forever. Amen."
1 John 5:20"He is the true God and eternal life."
Isaiah 9:6"For to us a child is born... and his name shall be called... Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace."
Matthew 1:23"Behold, the virgin shall conceive and bear a son, and they shall call his name Immanuel (which means, God with us)."

Jesus shares the divine attributes that belong to God alone.

Jesus is Worshiped as God​

Jesus accepted worship, which is reserved for God alone.

ReferenceVerse
Matthew 2:11"And going into the house, they saw the child with Mary his mother, and they fell down and worshiped him."
Matthew 14:33"And those in the boat worshiped him, saying, ‘Truly you are the Son of God.’"
John 9:38"He said, ‘Lord, I believe,’ and he worshiped him."
Revelation 5:13-14"To him who sits on the throne and to the Lamb be blessing and honor and glory and might forever and ever!"
Philippians 2:9-11"At the name of Jesus every knee should bow... and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord."
Luke 24:52"And they worshiped him and returned to Jerusalem with great joy."

Jesus Forgives Sins (Which Only God Can Do)​

The Bible teaches that only God has the authority to forgive sins (Isaiah 43:25, Mark 2:7). The Jewish leaders understood that claiming to forgive sins was an act of divine authority, yet Jesus repeatedly exercised this power, proving His deity. Additionally, Isaiah 53 and Psalm 22 prophesy the suffering servant who would bear the sins of the people, a role only God could fulfill.

Old Testament Foundation: God Alone Forgives Sins​

ReferenceVerse
Isaiah 43:25"I, I am he who blots out your transgressions for my own sake, and I will not remember your sins."
Psalm 103:12"As far as the east is from the west, so far does he remove our transgressions from us."

Jesus Explicitly Forgives Sins​

ReferenceVerse
Mark 2:5-7"'Son, your sins are forgiven.'... 'Who can forgive sins but God alone?'"
Luke 7:48-49"Then Jesus said to her, ‘Your sins are forgiven.’ And those who were at the table with him began to say among themselves, ‘Who is this, who even forgives sins?’"
Colossians 3:13"As the Lord has forgiven you, so you also must forgive."
Matthew 9:6"But that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins—he then said to the paralytic—'Rise, pick up your bed and go home.'"

The Suffering Servant: Jesus Bears Our Sins (Isaiah 53 & Psalm 22)​

The Old Testament prophesied that God Himself would provide atonement for sin. Jesus, the suffering servant, fulfilled these prophecies by taking upon Himself the punishment for sin.

ReferenceVerse
Isaiah 53:5"But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed."
Isaiah 53:6"All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned—every one—to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all."
Isaiah 53:12"Yet he bore the sin of many, and makes intercession for the transgressors."
Psalm 22:16-18"For dogs encompass me; a company of evildoers encircles me; they have pierced my hands and feet... They divide my garments among them, and for my clothing they cast lots."
Matthew 26:28"For this is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins."

Jesus is the Creator​

Jesus is not a created being; He is the Creator.

ReferenceVerse
John 1:3"All things were made through him, and without him was not anything made that was made."
Colossians 1:16-17"For by him all things were created... all things were created through him and for him."
Hebrews 1:2"Through whom also he created the world."
Revelation 3:14"The beginning of God’s creation."
Genesis 1:26"Then God said, ‘Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.’"

Jesus Calls Himself God​

Jesus directly identifies Himself as God.

ReferenceVerse
John 8:58"Before Abraham was, I am." (See Exodus 3:14)
John 10:30-33"I and the Father are one."
John 14:9"Whoever has seen me has seen the Father."
Mark 14:61-62"'Are you the Christ, the Son of the Blessed?' And Jesus said, ‘I am.’"
Revelation 22:13"I am the Alpha and the Omega, the first and the last, the beginning and the end."

Jesus is the Judge of All​

The Bible teaches that only God is the Judge, yet Jesus is given this role.

ReferenceVerse
John 5:22"For the Father judges no one, but has given all judgment to the Son."
2 Corinthians 5:10"For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ."
Acts 10:42"He is the one appointed by God to be judge of the living and the dead."
Matthew 25:31-32"When the Son of Man comes in his glory... he will separate people one from another."

🕊️ Key Insights​

These verses make it clear that Jesus is fully God:

  1. He is called God directly.
  2. He has the attributes of God.
  3. He is worshiped as God.
  4. He forgives sins (which only God can do).
  5. He created all things.
  6. He calls Himself God.
  7. He is the final Judge.
